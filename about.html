The Story

Where it all began

An artist working across multiple mediums — from the delicate transparency of watercolour to the rich depth of oil. Practice rooted in observation, emotion, and the quiet moments that often go unnoticed.

Art, for me, has always been about capturing what the eye sees but the camera cannot — the weight of a glance, the texture of light, the silence between forms.

"Practice is process-driven, observational, and rooted in inquiry rather than final outcomes."
Practice

The process

Whether it's a portrait that captures the soul behind the eyes, or a composition rendered in graphite, every piece of art deserves patience and honesty. I don't rush — each work is given the time it needs.

My practice engages with cultural contexts and the experimental use of materials to investigate form and space. Subjects range from the human figure to landscapes, from intimate sketches to large-format oil studies.
